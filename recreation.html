RECREATION PARKS

TAMAN BOTANI PUTRAJAYA

The 92-hectare Putrajaya Botanical Garden is one of the largest Botanical Gardens in Malaysia. Dubbed the "mother of all gardens", the Botanical Garden with the concept of "floral attractions" brings together more than 750 plant species from 13 collections of plant themes originating from different continents. It also houses the Moroccon Pavillion Islamic Garden which takes inspiration from Moroccan heritage and architecture. The park is divided into three main parts; Decorative Garden Division, Ethno-Botanical Garden Division and Conservation Research Division. The Decorative Garden Division consists of 13 thematic parks such as fern garden, sun garden and palm hill. The Botanical Garden is also popular as a destination for jogging, cycling and picnics.

TAMAN PUTRA PERDANA

The beginning of Putrajaya is enshrined here, from an oil palm plantation estate, Prang Besar to the administrative centre of the Federal Government. The 62.4-hectare Taman Putra Perdana which is located at the highest point in Precinct 1, not only acts as a buffer between the natural zone in the north with offices and commercial spaces in the South and East but also the storage of official documents and the history of Putrajaya's construction embedded in the Time Capsule.

The main attraction of the park is the Putrajaya Landmark, the first monument built to celebrate the country's achievements in conjunction with the establishment of Putrajaya in 1995. Two iconic buildings - Melawati Palace and Grande Pulse Hotel are also located here. Other attractions include a herb garden, jogging track, flower garden and gazebo.

TAMAN WAWASAN PUTRAJAYA

The 137-acre park is located in a slightly elevated Precinct 2. The main attraction is the Metropolitan Park as its high level allows visitors to enjoy the overall view of Putrajaya from the Reviewing Platform provided. Within the park is the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Wisma Putra) which is the Federal Government Complex.

Besides that, Taman Wawasan also has Aqua Plaza (Plaza Air), a comfortable and conducive amphitheatre for any program< and activities as well as a beautifully structured Pantai Kerikil (Pebble Beach). Various recreational activities are suitable to be carried out in Taman Wawasan such as jogging, picnicking, cycling and so on.

TAMAN WETLAND PUTRAJAYA

Located in Precinct 13, this 3.35 sq km public park was developed with the aim of maintaining greenery as well as as a 'Green Lung' in the development of Putrajaya. Unlike the other 90 wetlands in the country, Putrajaya Wetland Park is the country's first man-made freshwater wetland, which includes 24 cells and wetlands. The beauty and greenery of Wetland Park has successfully made it home to over 59 species of migratory birds and 200 species of local birds as well as over 24 species of freshwater fish and aquatic animals.

Wetland Park provides a variety of facilities that attract visitors such as Flamingo Pond, Menara Pandang and the latest Glamping Taman Wetland suitable for a variety of activities including cycling, jogging, fishing and kayaking as well as camping activities, family days, nature exploration and so on.

TAMAN SAUJANA HIJAU

Premises on the concept of “Salem High Country”, Saujana Hijau Park is a popular destination, Saujana Hijau Park is surrounded by pine and cyprus trees, gives a feeling of being in a “high country” in Europe. Saujana Hijau Park is surrounded by Gymnospermae plants such as pine trees, juniper trees, thuja pines and cycads plant from Queensland, Australia and many more. Within the park are 3 themed gardens namely European Garden, Chinese Garden and English Garden. Wide walkways for running and bicycle lanes encircle the park. Visitors can enjoy the morning dew, watch the birds of prey bask in the afternoon sun and take in the gentle breeze. It is a popular destination for activities such as jogging, picnicking, wedding photography and more.
